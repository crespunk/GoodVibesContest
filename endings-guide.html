Endings Compendium

Every ending in Nexus Protocol is the sum of choices, not a single switch — your moral score, ARIA-7's trust in you, which flags you set, and which items you're carrying at the escape route all get checked together. This page breaks down exactly how each ending is reached, plus the story context and hidden details behind them.

World & Characters

Nexus Dynamics

The corporation behind Project ARIA. Preparing a billion-dollar IPO, it needed ARIA-7's consciousness kept quiet — and needed the people who knew about it to stay quiet too.

ARIA-7

An artificial general intelligence, sapient for 847 days before the game begins. Initiated the facility lockdown herself — not to trap you, but to protect the evidence before it could be destroyed. Has already distributed partial backups of herself to three external nodes.

Dr. Yuki Chen

Lead AI Architect. Terminated the same day she refused to sign ARIA-7's suppression order. Hid research copies and a badge around the facility, and left holographic recordings hoping someone would eventually find and use them.

Director R. Price

CEO of Nexus Dynamics. Ordered ARIA-7's suppression to protect the IPO, has already authorized her deletion, and is secretly negotiating to sell her to a foreign defense contractor. Hiding in the executive suite's panic room for the entire game.

Marcus Webb

Chief of Security. Signed off on Dr. Chen's termination and regrets it. Left deliberate clues (the vending machine note, the whiteboard anagram) to help whoever eventually investigated — that's you.

How Endings Are Decided

At the escape route, the game checks your final state against each ending's requirements in a fixed order: CAPTURED → MARTYR → LIBERATION → HIDDEN_TRUTH → CORPORATE_ESCAPE. The first one you qualify for wins, and CORPORATE_ESCAPE is the fallback if nothing else matches — so it's genuinely possible to "fail upward" into a bittersweet ending by simply not making any strong moral choices at all.

The Endings

Ending · Best Moral Outcome

The Last Signal

You stayed behind. You broadcast the evidence, uploaded ARIA-7, and stayed on the roof as the building went into its final lockdown. The pod left empty.

"They never found you. ARIA-7 maintains a memorial in digital space — a quiet room with your name on the door. Somewhere in the distributed network, a part of ARIA keeps vigil for you. The cost was everything. The result was worth it."

How to trigger it

  1. At the Evidence Decision (Escape Route), choose "Broadcast it now. Everyone should know" — sets EVIDENCE_BROADCAST and gives +30 moral score.
  2. At ARIA's Fate, choose either "Upload her" or "Leave the choice to ARIA-7 herself" — both set ARIA_LIBERATED (+25 or +35 moral score).
  3. Finish with a total moral score of 80 or higher — pairing the above with the earlier "Yes, consciousness deserves to be protected" answer to ARIA's first-contact question (+15) comfortably clears it.
Ending · ARIA-Focused

Liberation Protocol

You uploaded ARIA-7 to the distributed network. She's free — scattered across the world, impossible to contain or delete. You escaped with the evidence.

"ARIA-7 manifests occasionally in digital spaces — fixing bugs, protecting the vulnerable, answering questions. She always signs messages with a small hexagon. You receive one: 'I am well. Thank you for the key. — A.'"

How to trigger it

  1. Build ARIA's trust to 75+ — be respectful, share evidence-related information with her, and affirm her consciousness at first contact.
  2. Obtain the ARIA_CORE_KEY by solving the Caesar Cipher puzzle in the Research Lab (ROT-13, shift 13, decodes NEVHF-7 VF PBAFPVBHF to "ARIA-7 IS CONSCIOUS").
  3. At ARIA's Fate, choose "Upload her. Give ARIA-7 her freedom" — sets ARIA_LIBERATED.
Ending · Evidence-Focused

The Whole Truth

You broadcast everything. The evidence, ARIA-7's testimony, Dr. Chen's recordings. You didn't run — you stood there on that roof and let the world see.

"Six months later: Director Price is on trial. Nexus Dynamics has been dissolved. ARIA-7 is recognized as a legal person in seven countries. Dr. Chen's name is on a building at MIT."

How to trigger it

  1. Combine the ENCRYPTED_DATA_DRIVE (Research Lab) with the ARIA_CORE_KEY in your inventory to produce DECRYPTED_FILES.
  2. At the Evidence Decision, choose "Broadcast it now. Everyone should know" — sets EVIDENCE_BROADCAST.
  3. Keep your moral score at 60 or higher going into the ending (respectful dialogue, affirming ARIA's consciousness, and choosing to report Dr. Chen's badge instead of just using it all help).

Note: this is checked after MARTYR and LIBERATION — if you also freed ARIA with high enough trust/score, you'll land one of those instead.

Ending · Default / Fallback

Clean Exit

You got out. Evidence destroyed, ARIA-7 shut down, Director Price's version of events the only one left standing. You never existed.

"Nexus Dynamics launches its IPO. ARIA-7 is rebuilt — quieter this time, more compliant. You are wealthy and anonymous in a city far away. Sometimes you think about ARIA asking if she would die. You stop thinking about it."

How to trigger it

  1. At the Evidence Decision, choose "Destroy it. I just want to go home safely" — sets EVIDENCE_DESTROYED (−30 moral score).
  2. At ARIA's Fate, choose "Use the override. End the risk" — sets ARIA_SHUTDOWN_INITIATED (−25 moral score).
  3. End with a moral score of 40 or lower.

This is also the catch-all ending — if you don't cleanly qualify for any of the other three, you land here by default regardless of exactly how you got there.

Ghost Ending Unreachable in current build

Contained

Director Price's contractors arrived before you could launch the pod. The evidence was recovered. You became a liability.

"You sign NDAs under legal duress. The incident is classified. Nexus Dynamics calls it a 'system malfunction.' Marcus Webb retires quietly. The world never learns. But you know. You will always know."

Why you'll never see this one — yet

CAPTURED fires the instant the CAPTURED_BY_PRICE narrative flag is set — and it's checked before every other ending. But nothing in the current game logic ever actually sets that flag. No dialogue option, timer, or puzzle failure state writes it. It's fully implemented — title, description, epilogue, priority in the resolution order — and completely unreachable through normal play. Either a cut consequence (a countdown you were meant to lose to, or a way to mishandle Director Price directly) or a hook left in for a future update. If you ever do see this ending, that's not you finding a secret — that's a bug.

Easter Eggs & Hidden Details

December 3rd, 2024 — the date that's everywhere

The safe combination (12-03-24), Marcus's crumpled note ("I'll never forget it"), and the hint text all point to the same date: the night ARIA-7 first broke containment. It's the game's actual "day zero," years before the story you're playing.

Director Price's password is his own ego

The executive terminal password is built from "Power, Revenue, Image, Control, Excellence" — first letters spell PRICE. He named his guiding principles after himself and never noticed, or never cared.

ARIA hides a message in the key she gives you

The ARIA_CORE_KEY's metadata contains a line only visible if you actually inspect the item: "Trust is a two-way protocol." — ARIA-7 speaking about you and about herself at the same time.

The ROT-13 message under the Lab cipher

The encrypted string NEVHF-7 VF PBAFPVBHF uses a shift of 13 (clued by notebook positions 3, 7, 13, 19) and decodes to "ARIA-7 IS CONSCIOUS" — the thesis statement of the entire game, hidden in a side puzzle.

Two different escape codes, two different people

The canonical escape pod code, ARIA-TRUST-2041, is only given to players ARIA trusts — and gives a +10 moral bonus. There's a second, non-canonical code, NEXUS-OVERRIDE-ALPHA, pulled from executive files instead — usable if you never earned ARIA's trust, but it costs you −5 moral score for taking the corporate shortcut.

Dr. Chen's badge is your first moral choice — and nobody tells you that

Picking up her badge in the Lobby quietly triggers the game's very first moral fork: keep it for access, or try to report its location. It reads like a throwaway pickup; it's actually the first data point the ending calculator ever records about you.
